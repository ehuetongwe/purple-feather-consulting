Why Full Stack

The problem with building them separately.

Most businesses end up with a website built by one person and a backend configured by another. The result is two systems that look disconnected, behave inconsistently, and require two separate people to maintain. Full Stack eliminates that problem entirely. One consultant builds both sides and makes sure they talk to each other from day one.

Without Full Stack

Your form submits but nothing routes to your CRM. Your checkout processes but invoices don't send. Your calendar books but confirmations don't trigger.

With Full Stack

Every touchpoint is connected. A visitor books → your CRM updates → an invoice sends → a confirmation emails → your dashboard reflects it. All of it. Automatically.

The Integration Layer

This is the work neither Builds nor Systems covers alone. It's the configuration that makes both sides aware of each other — and it's included in every Full Stack engagement.

The Process

How a Full Stack engagement works.

01

Discovery

We map your brand, your goals, your existing tools, and your operational gaps. We need the full picture before we build anything.

02

Architecture

We design both the front-end experience and the backend systems map before writing a single line of code.

03

Build

We build the website and the backend in parallel so integration decisions are made during the build, not bolted on after.

04

We connect every touchpoint and test every flow end to end. Forms, payments, automations, calendar — nothing ships until it all works.

05

Launch & Handoff

We deploy, walk you through everything you own, and hand off full documentation. You leave with a system that runs itself.
